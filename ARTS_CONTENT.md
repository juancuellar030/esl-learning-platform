Part 1: The Essential Verb List (Extracted & Adapted)
These verbs correspond directly to the "Desempeños" (Performances) and "Saber Hacer" (Knowing how to do) sections of the syllabus.
A. Visual Arts (Drawing & Painting)
Found in syllabus: Dibujar, pintar, colorear, mezclar, ilustrar, trazar.
1.	Draw: (General term) "Draw a circle."
2.	Sketch: (For the "bocetos" mentioned in Grade 1 & 5) "Sketch the outline lightly."
3.	Paint: (Using brushes/tempera) "Paint the background blue."
4.	Color: (Using pencils/crayons) "Color inside the lines."
5.	Mix / Blend: (For primary/secondary colors in Grades 1, 2, 3, 4) "Mix yellow and blue to make green."
6.	Trace: (For the "grid/cuadrícula" technique in Grade 5) "Trace the image onto the paper."
7.	Shade: (For "tonalidades" in Grade 4) "Shade the darker areas."
8.	Outline: "Outline your drawing with a black marker."
B. Crafts & Construction (Manualidades & Recycling)
Found in syllabus: Elaborar, construir, recortar, pegar, modelar, material reciclable.
9. Cut (out): "Cut out the shape." / "Cut along the dotted line."
10. Glue / Paste: "Glue the cardboard to the paper."
11. Fold: "Fold the paper in half."
12. Stick: "Stick the pieces together."
13. Recycle: "We are going to recycle these bottles."
14. Assemble / Put together: (For puppets/marotes in Grades 2, 3, 5) "Let's assemble the puppet."
15. Decorate: (For the Christmas crafts mentioned in Period IV) "Decorate your ornament."
C. Performing Arts (Dance & Theater)
Found in syllabus: Danza, expresión corporal, mímica, títeres, teatro de sombras.
16. Move: "Move your body to the rhythm."
17. Imitate / Copy: (For "mímica" in Grade 4) "Imitate my movements."
18. Act: "Act like a happy person."
19. Listen: (Essential for the rhythm exercises in Grades 2, 3, 4, 5) "Listen to the beat."
20. Freeze: (Great for classroom management in dance) "Dance... and freeze!"
21. Stretch: (For the warm-up/calentamiento sections) "Stretch your arms up."

Part 2: Essential Classroom Commands (Imperatives)
Group these by the "Moments" of the class mentioned in the PDF (Exploration, Structuring, Practice, Valuation).
1. Preparation & Setup (Exploration/Contextualization)
•	"Put on your apron/smock." (Essential for painting classes).
•	"Clear your desks."
•	"Take out your..." (pencil case, sketchbook, materials).
•	"Gather your materials." (Referring to the "recursos" section).
•	"Come to the carpet/circle." (For younger kids/storytelling).
2. Instruction & Demonstration (Structuring)
•	"Watch me first."
•	"Pay attention to the colors." (Psychology of color - Grade 3).
•	"Follow my steps."
•	"Raise your hand if you have a question."
•	"Look at the board."
3. Doing the Work (Practice/Execution)
•	"Be careful with the scissors." (Safety).
•	"Don't rush, take your time." (Encouraging "Saber Ser/Sentir").
•	"Share with your partner." (Teamwork/Saber Convivir).
•	"Use a little bit of glue." (Resource management).
•	"Be creative!" / "Use your imagination." (A key "Propósito de formación").
4. Clean Up & Review (Valuation/Evidences)
•	"Cap your markers/glue."
•	"Wash your brushes."
•	"Clean your workspace."
•	"Put the scraps in the recycling bin." (Environment transversal project).
•	"Show me your work."
•	"Let's display our art." (For the "Exposición de trabajos" mentioned in the strategies).

Part 3: Specific Vocabulary Nouns (From the PDF)
Reviewing these nouns will help her build the sentences above.
•	Materials: Tempera paint, watercolors, brushes, colored pencils, markers, scissors, glue stick, liquid glue, cardboard, recyclables (bottles, caps, paper), clay/playdough, apron.
•	Techniques: Collage, mixed media (técnica mixta), grid (cuadrícula), sketch (boceto), landscape, portrait.
•	Performing Arts: Puppet (títere), sock puppet (títere de media), stick puppet (marote), mask (máscara), shadow (sombra), stage/theater (teatrín), script (guion), rhythm (ritmo), folk dance (danza folclórica).
•	Elements of Art: Primary colors, secondary colors, warm colors (cálidos), cool colors (fríos), shape (forma), line (línea), texture (textura).
Suggested Activity for Your Lesson: "Simon Says Art"
To help her memorize the commands, play a role-reversal game where she is the teacher and gives you the commands based on a specific grade level scenario.
Scenario A (Grade 1): "Teach me how to mix primary colors to make orange."
•	Target commands: Take the yellow paint. Take the red paint. Mix them together. Look! It is orange.
Scenario B (Grade 5): "Instruct me on how to set up the Shadow Theater (Teatro de Sombras)."
•	Target commands: Turn off the lights. Turn on the lamp. Hold the puppet behind the screen. Move the puppet.
Scenario C (All Grades): "The Clean Up Song."
•	Target commands: Stop working. Wash your hands. Pick up the paper. Sit down quietly.
